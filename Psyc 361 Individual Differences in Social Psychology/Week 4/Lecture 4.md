Self-Determination Theory | Jun 5

Classical model:
- utilizes operant conditioning to increase the likelihood of a behaviour repeating by following it with a positive stimulus or the removal of a negative stimulus
Types of motivation:
- Autonomous vs controlled
	- Autonomous/self-determined motivation: includes motivation from internal sources and feeling a full sense of volition, freedom, and choice.
		- individuals feel self-directed
	- Controlled/non-self-determined motivation: comprised of external regulation - where individuals behave due to external rewards or fear of punishment
		- individuals feel controlled
- Intrinsic vs extrinsic
	- Intrinsic: a drive to behave in certain ways based on personal rewards
		- core values, interests, enjoyment, personal morality
	- Extrinsic motivation: drive to behave based on external sources; to earn reward or avoid punishment
		- can be both autonomous and controlled: if people internalize values or importance is placed on extrinsic sources of motivation, they can be extrinsically motivated but also autonomously motivated
- the motivation that underlies the goals we set impacts out broader well being
	- intrinsic goals: physical health, self-acceptance, affiliation
	- extrinsic: wealth, fame, beauty
Basic psychological needs
- Autonomy
	- to act volitionally and in accordance with one's internal values and goals
- Competence
	- Sense of efficacy and competence in one's context. Not just about having the skills, but opportunities to improve
- Relatedness
	- Feeling cared for, connected to, and experiencing a sense of belonging with others

- When psychological needs are met > healthy development, psychological well-being, effective performance
- When they are not met > unhealthy development, lower wellbeing
Optimal motivation
- the more people perceive tasks as fulfilling psychological needs, the more the task is perceived as enjoyable
	- intrinsic or more autonomous motivation likely
- the more people perceive tasks as thwarting their psychological needs, the more the task is perceived as unenjoyable
	- extrinsic motivation or amotivation likely
Need based support
- supporting behaviours from others influence peoples' need satisfaction and foster wellbeing
- Observed in all sorts of relational contexts, from workplaces to intimate relationships

- Conditions that facilitate need satisfaction
	- Autonomy-relevant
		- absence of pressure
		- providing rationale
		- offering meaningful choices
		- perspective-taking
	- Competence-relevant
		- optimal challenge
		- informational feedback
	- Relatedness-relevant
		- empathy
		- warmth
		- acknowledgement of emotions
- Conditions that undermine need satisfaction
	- Autonomy-relevant
		- pressure toward outcomes/deadlines
		- punishment or reward contingencies
		- controlling language
		- surveillance
	- Competence-relevant
		- non-optimal challenges
		- negative feedback
	- Relatedness-relevant
		- "cold" interactions
		- isolation
		- criticism
