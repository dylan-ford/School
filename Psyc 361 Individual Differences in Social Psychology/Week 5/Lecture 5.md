Regulatory Mode
- Motivation so far has covered approach and avoidance goals, intrinsic/extrinsic motives has come from a pain perspective; things people are trying to avoid/achieve
- Regulatory mode theory aims to move beyond please and pain distinctions
- Focuses on **how people approach situations to achieve their goals**
- Depression and anxiety are both painful experiences
	- how they manifest in action are different. Depression makes it difficult to obtain goals because it prevents motivation for any action
	- anxiety prevents goal obtainment by fixating on rumination and negative thoughts
- Theory is about how goals are pursued and how problems are solved

- Locomotion orientation: focus on moving and getting things done (do it now)
	- people high in locomotion report: greater positive affect, higher self esteem and optimism, fewer depressive symptoms
- Assessment orientation: about doing something right; compare different goals and analyze options (do it right)
	- people report greater negative affect, lower self-esteem and optimism, greater depressive symptoms
- Depressive moods were most evident in individuals who were low on locomotion but high on assessment, suggesting that critical self-evaluation coupled with a sense of non-progression towards goas was extremely depressing
- Locomotion orientation is associated with intrinsic motivation
- Assessment orientation associated with extrinsic motivation (how would people think if i did x?)
- The attribute of grit is positively associated with locomotion, as well as consistent interest and persevere effort
- People high in locomotion were more likely to experience better support relevant personal and relational outcomes, because they perceived movement toward their goal
- Summary
	- comparing assessment orientation to locomotive, some of the reasons that locomotion orientation is linked to better outcomes (well being, goal attainment) is because its associated with intrinsic motivation, which drives harmonious forms of passion. 
	- Locomotive orientation is associated with greater grit and perseverance when experience setbacks, better time management, less distractions, less procrastination, more felt control of time; progress
Task complexity
- high locomotion on its own is sufficient for simple tasks
- high locomotion + high assessment both required for complex tasks
Short term vs long term goals
- people primes with either locomotion orientation or assessment orientation (monetary delay task - smaller amount of money now vs more money later)
- people in the assessment condition made far-sighted choices compared to people in the locomotive condition - tipping point for choosing future/delay rewards was at 25% higher than the immediate reward
	- priming locomotion decreased likelihood to delay gratification
Speed vs accuracy goals
- all locomotive group (group members high in locomotion) had fastest decision time
- same findings for mixed groups; locomotive people help an entire group reduce indecision
- all assessor group required more time to formulate a decision
	- also the most accurate
	- mixed groups were also most accurate
- summary
	- assessment orientation can be associated with benefits when goal related outcomes are complicated, people need to consider long term task, AND when being accurate is super important 
- Regulatory mode is equally important in how we support others with their goals
	- assessment orientation can help them reframe their issues
	- research shows teachers high in locomotion orientation prefer providing autonomy support
	- teachers high in assessment orientation prefer providing more controlled teaching
- Regulatory fit
	- although people choose a manner of pursuit that fits their own orientation, people with power over them determine how they pursue that goal
	- cannot just provide a 'one size fits all' support
	- Autonomy supportive learning environment is most satisfying for students high in locomotion orientation
	- Controlled learning environment is most satisfying for students high in assessment orientation
