• Who are the Southern Saami?
- indigenous groups in Norway and Sweden who's culture revolved around reindeer herding
• In what ways have they had their Indigenous Rights violated?
- their self-determination is inhibited by state border. their presence has been historically negated by an "authorized truth" that they arrived late to their lands, which has been used to to deny lands rights in court. current wind energy projects dispossess them of pastures
• The Southern Saami are negatively affected by climate change, so why don’t they support
wind turbines on their territory?
- wind turbines represent "green colonialism" because they bring infrastructure and human activity that destroys pasturelands essential for reindeer handling and Saami cultural survival
• In addition to an economic threat, how do threats to reindeer herding affect the southern Saami? Culture, identity, spirituality.
- Reindeer are seen as "big family" and reindeer is the last remaining space for transferring language and knowledge. Losing herding is viewed as a form of "cultural genocide" because the community would "disappear" without it. It is a practice of freedom and love for the animals.
• How do the Southern Saami connect wind power projects to their past?
- wind projects are a continuation of piece -by-piece politics and historical depress - look into more of this
- protestors anchor their current struggle in memories of the 1970s Alta struggle. using the slogan "let the moutains live" to callback to the old slogan "let the river live"

• Why do Southern Saami continue to resist, despite the challenges?
