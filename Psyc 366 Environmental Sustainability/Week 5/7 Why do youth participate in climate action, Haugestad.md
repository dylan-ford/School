• What is the “Norwegian Paradox”?
- describes the contradiction in Norway where the government portrays ambitious climate leadership and strong pro-environment goals while simultaneously managing policies to sustain a robust fossil fuel industry which provides the country's wealth and sustainability
• What kind of study was Study 1? Who were the participants?
- qualitative, ethnographic investigation of protestors in Oslo
• What three themes emerged from the interviews in Study 1? Describe the content of each theme.
- negotiating responsibility for climate change
	- participants viewed climate change as a structural problem caused by economic and technological systems. they advocated for collective responsibility and government intervention over individual blame/scapegoating
- timely action being required to save our future:
	- youth felt cheater of the future they were promised and see cliate change as an imminent, visible threat. this deadline narrative created a sense of urgency
- Shared identity motivates protest:
	- participants collectively identified as "the youth" or "the neglected future". This shared identity facilitated a sense of community and the belief that striking was their only effective tool at influencing policies 
• What kind of study was Study 2? Who were the participants?
- a qualitative survey designed to test and extend to the Social Identity Model of Collective Action (SIMCA)
