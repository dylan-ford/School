Lecture 1
- "Tragedy of the commons; dilemma"
	- states that people sharing a resource that is limited
	- assumes people act rationally and in their individual self-interest
	- people see own impact as trivial
	- when unsustainability noticed, people try to consume more before others get what is left
	- no communication
	- solutions
		- externa coercion (gov. regulation)
		- privatization (end commons)
- Social psychology critique
	- humans don't always act as rational, self0interested individuals (context and variation)
	- imagining group discussion increases cooperation, as does actual communication
	- collective identity facilitates cooperation through shared norms, trust, and reciprocity
		- leading to sustaining resources
	- individualistic models of human nature make sustainability seem impossible
Lecture 2
- 99% scientific consensus that global warming is caused by human efforts
- warmer weather increases hurricanes, typhoons, high precipitation events, droughts, fires
	- food scarcity, rising sea levels, biodiversity extinction, geopolitical conflict, ocean acidification
- environmental threats are psychologically threatening
	- cause fear, anxiety, guilt, sadness
	- Feelings of lack of control, powerlessness, existential crisis, threats to worldview (just world belief)
- sources of concern:
	- egoistic: concern for self
	- altruistic: concern for other people
	- biospheric: concern for other species, ecosystems
- Solastalgia: distress produced by environmental change impacting on people while they are directly connected to their home environment
- threats to psychological well-being from climate change effects:
	- loss of loved ones, physical health, infrastructure breakdown, food insecurity, dislocation
	- extreme events causing trauma, long term uncertainty
- groups at greatest risk
	- low SES
	- global south
	- racialized minorities
	- indigenous
	- people with disabilities
	- women
	- children
- Strategies for coping with environmental problems
	- Disengagement vs engagement
	- Denial/avoidance doesn't address problem which lets sources of underlying negative affect persist 
	- taking action WITH other people gives a sense of efficacy and feeling like you can make actual change, which helps reduce feelings of climate anxiety
