• What did Passmore et al do in this study?
- Randomly assigned undergraduate students to one of three conditions:
	- paying attention to how nature in their everyday surroundings made them feel
	- paying attention to how human-built objects in their everyday surroundings made them feel
	- business as usual
- goal is to find how our relationship with nature plays a role in peoples' well-being
	- contact with nature has been shown to enhance a variety of aspects of well-being
- 3 scales were used to assess well-being
	- positive vs negative affect
	- feeling of elevation
	- meaning
	- general sense of connectedness as well as prosocial orientation were measured
• What effects did they find on different outcomes?
- participants in the nature condition did not spend significantly more time in nature over the course of the two weeks than did participants in either the human-built or control conditions
